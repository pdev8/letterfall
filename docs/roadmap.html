E7The Living Deck ★ core0/9M3
DB-186Backend foundation on Supabase (seeds, submissions, move logs, usage aggregates; anon auth + RLS)Lpost-launch

The server foundation for everything social: daily seeds, score submissions, move logs, and word-usage aggregates, with anonymous device accounts and strict row-level access rules. Validated leaderboards, word retirement, and the weekly meta all build on this.

DB-177Dynamic bays — park on any empty column, max 3 parked (sim-proven fix for the left-first trap; rule change, no generator needed)Mready early

A rule change proven in simulation: park the reserve card on any empty column (with at most 3 parked cards on the board) instead of only the left three bays. It kills the trap where clearing the left side first felt smart but quietly wrecked your endgame, and it lets you stage a long closing word by parking into emptied columns.

DB-170On-device seeded generator: board shapes, distribution + altitude guards, opennessMtodo

The deal generator moves onto your phone: from a seed it builds a board that starts with a proven winning line, guards the letter mix (no duplicate floods, rare letters surface early, vowel-friendly column bottoms), and rejects boards that don't offer plenty of playable words. Same seed always produces the same deal — that's what makes shared daily games possible.

DB-171Solvability measurement solver — par, openness, "possible until move N"Ltodo

A background checker that can tell whether a board can still be finished. It's used to measure — estimating a deal's par score, scoring how open a board is, and powering the post-game insight "a win was still possible until move 14" after a loss — never to bail the player out of a bad position.

DB-172Draw-time stock steering — f(seed, move history), fairness guards + warmth curveLtodo

The undrawn stock doesn't exist until you draw: the next card is computed on the spot from the seed and your moves, so there's nothing stored for cheat tools to peek at. Steering enforces fairness — duplicate caps so you never see seven E's, a guaranteed vowel when you're starved — with a warmth curve that makes a deal's early draws kinder and goes neutral by mid-game. It never rescues a lost position: dead is dead, on your line, not the shuffle.

DB-173Difficulty ramp: shape + generosity + recycles + max-parked per game 1–5Mtodo

The daily set's difficulty dial: across games 1–5, board shape steepens, draw generosity tightens, and recycles and parking shrink — so game 1 is a warm-up and game 5 is the test. Also decides the daily-set score multiplier.

DB-174Daily set mode — 5 scored games, shared seeds, daily total, countdownMtodo

The scored mode: five games a day, the same deals for every player, adding up to one daily total with a reset countdown. Scarcity makes the total mean something; free play stays unlimited and unscored.

DB-175Par estimation + score bandsMtodo

The generator estimates what score each deal realistically supports (its par) and rejects deals outside a target band. That keeps deal luck off the leaderboard — daily totals compare skill, not shuffle fortune.

DB-176Seed service: local hash now, server-issued + replay validation designedMtodo

How scored games get their seeds: at launch they're derived on the device from the date, so everyone plays the same set. The phase-2 design — server-issued seeds plus move-log replay validation, the real anti-cheat — is documented here and built later on DB-186.

Design reference

Scoring letter values · multipliers · Encore

Rewards hard letters, long words, word economy, stock discipline — and the closer.

letters 1: aeioulnstr 2: dg 3: bcmp 4: fhvwy 5: k 8: jx 10: qz wordScore = round( Σ letterValue × lengthMult ) lengthMult 3:1.00 4:1.25 5:1.60 6:2.00 7:2.50 8:3.20 Encore the FINAL word of a winning deal scores ×2 dealScore = round( base × wordEconomy × stockEconomy × difficulty ) wordEconomy ≤4:1.75 5:1.60 6:1.45 7:1.30 8:1.20 9:1.10 10:1.05 11+:1.00 stockEconomy clamp(1.5 − 0.08·reserveUsed − 0.05·parks − 0.08·recycles, 1.0, 1.5) difficulty Casual ×1.00 · Standard ×1.25 · Expert ×1.60

Only wins bank points. Total points = Σ banked deal scores; daily total = Σ the day's 5 games.

Players never see this math. In-game it's named bonus chips — "Word Economy +30%", "Stock Discipline +21%", "PURIST ★", "ENCORE ×2" — tallied on the win screen; the rulebook explains them in plain language, with exact tables behind a fine-print link.

The Living Deck generation v2 — the core
  • Infinite, low-repeat: seeded deals at play time; no pool, no file to mine.
  • Fair start, honest finish: every deal starts winnable — a witnessed winning line exists at generation and CI replays it forever — but squander the position and dead is dead, on your line, not the shuffle.
  • Par band: deals are generated into a target achievable-score band, so daily totals compare skill, not deal luck.
  • The undrawn stock doesn't exist yet: next card = f(seed, move history) at draw time — kills letter floods and lookahead tools.
  • Steering enforces fairness, not victory: distribution guards (duplicate caps, vowel lifeline) plus a warmth curve — early draws steered kinder to get the game moving, neutral within guards by mid-game; it never resurrects a lost position.
  • Measure, don't rescue: the solver's runtime job is measurement — par estimation, openness metrics, and the post-game "a win was still possible until move N" insight — never guiding play or bailing the player out.
  • Board geometry (sim-proven): shapes ramp flat [4×7] → steep as the daily difficulty lever; heights position-shuffled; rare letters surface early; dynamic bays repair endgame breadth.
  • Daily set: 5 shared scored games/day — the only publicly ranked mode; free play is private.
Modes & leaderboards public daily · private free play
  • Daily Challenge (public): Game Center boards — daily total, best word of the week, all-time points.
  • Free play (private): unlimited, feeds personal stats: time played, games, avg time/game, letters constructed, unique words, most-played top 10, bests, streaks.
  • Anti-cheat: client seeds at launch; server-issued seeds + move-log replay validation in M5.
Word ladder the living meta — post-launch

Daily-challenge results advance a ranked tier; higher tiers retire the community's most-played words. The ban list is recomputed each play day from the trailing week's usage and lifts the next day — the meta breathes, never strangles. The generator plays against each tier's effective lexicon, so every deal stays winnable without the banned words.
