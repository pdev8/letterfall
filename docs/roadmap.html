Spec · core

The Living Deck

Six pillars, from docs/GENERATION.md:

Infinite, low-repeat
Deals generated from seeds at play time — no pool to exhaust, no file to memorize
Solvable, in a par band
A win and a good score always reachable; deal-to-deal score ceilings kept in a narrow band so totals compare skill, not luck
Difficulty ramps
Daily game 1 is generous, game 5 is tight — via steering generosity + rule tightening, never by breaking solvability
5 games, one daily total
Scored mode is a shared daily set; scarcity makes the total mean something; free play unlimited, unscored
The undrawn stock doesn't exist yet
Next card computed at draw time = f(seed, move history): kills letter floods ("seven E's"), kills lookahead tools — there's nothing stored to peek at
Guarantee, don't guide
Steering keeps some path alive but never hints toward it — the possibility space stays the player's

Word score

wordScore = round( Σ letterValue × lengthMult ) length 3 4 5 6 7 8 lengthMult 1.00 1.25 1.60 2.00 2.50 3.20

Deliberately super-linear: one six-letter word beats two threes made of the same letters. QUIZ = (10+1+1+10) × 1.25 = 28; CAT = 5.

Deal score — awarded only on a win

dealScore = round( baseScore × wordEconomyMult × stockEconomyMult × difficultyMult ) baseScore = Σ wordScore over the winning deal words used ≤4 5 6 7 8 9 10 11+ wordEconomyMult 1.75 1.60 1.45 1.30 1.20 1.10 1.05 1.00 stockEconomyMult = clamp( 1.5 − 0.08·reserveLettersPlayed − 0.05·parksUsed − 0.08·recyclesUsed, 1.0, 1.5 )

Drawing is free — browsing the stock costs nothing; consuming it does. A clear with zero stock letters, zero parks, and zero recycles keeps the full 1.5 — the Purist bonus. Difficulty multiplies last: Casual ×1.00, Standard ×1.25, Expert ×1.60.

Worked example

Standard difficulty, cleared in 7 words, word scores summing to 240, using 3 reserve letters, 1 park, 0 recycles:

stockEconomyMult = 1.5 − 0.24 − 0.05 = 1.21 dealScore = round( 240 × 1.30 × 1.21 × 1.25 ) = 472
Encore — the closer scores double

The final word of a winning deal earns 2× its wordScore. Word lengths structurally fade as columns empty (sim-proven); Encore plus dynamic bays turns the ending into a planned climax — hold breadth, stage a long closer — instead of a 3-letter grind.

Dead or abandoned deals bank no points (their words still count toward achievements). Total points — the leaderboard currency — is the sum of banked deal scores.

Modes & Leaderboards

Daily Challenge is the public game — the 5-game daily set is the only mode on global boards (Game Center: daily total, best word of the week, all-time challenge points). Same seed chain for everyone, so ranks compare like-for-like. The community's most-used words of the week are surfaced in-app — and feed the ladder's daily-reset retirement.

Free play is private — unlimited deals, player-picked difficulty, never ranked publicly. It feeds the personal dashboard: total time played, games and wins, average time per game, letters constructed, unique words, most-played words, bests, streaks — tracked per mode.

Post-launch, the word ladder (E8) turns challenge results into a ranked tier — and the higher the tier, the more of the community's most-played words are retired. Known limitation: scores are client-computed at launch; server seeds + move-log replay validation is the M5 hardening path.

Epic · post-launch

E8Word Ladder & Living Meta

Ranked progression where the community's crutch words retire out of the game: climb via daily-challenge results; at higher tiers the most-played words are banned, driven by real usage across all players. Retirement resets each play day — the day's ban list is recomputed from the trailing week's most-used words and applies for that day only. The living deck generates against the player's effective lexicon, so every deal stays winnable without that day's banned words.
